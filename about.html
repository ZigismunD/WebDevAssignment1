History of programming

Programming has in a sense existed for centuries. In the 9th century, programmable music sequencer was invented by the Banu Musa brothers, who described an automated flute player. So programmable devices has existed for a long time, even though commonly people associate programming only with the modern computer programming.

The first computer program is dated to 1843, when mathematician Ada Lovelace created and published algorithm to calculate a sequence of Bernoulli numbers, intended to be carried out by Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine.
